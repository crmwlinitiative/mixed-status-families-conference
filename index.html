About the Symposium

A Critical Conversation at a Defining Moment

Millions of American families include members with different immigration statuses — citizens, permanent residents, DACA recipients, visa holders, and undocumented loved ones living side by side. Today, these families face unprecedented challenges as enforcement policies reshape the landscape of belonging in the United States.

This symposium brings together nationally recognized scholars, legal practitioners, journalists, and community leaders to examine how immigration enforcement impacts mixed-status families — and how communities are responding with resilience, advocacy, and legal innovation.

Convened by the Civil Rights, Migration and Workplace Law Initiative at ASU Law, this full-day event offers a rare opportunity to engage with leading voices at the intersection of immigration law, civil rights, and family life in America.

Keynote Speaker
Professor César Cuauhtémoc García Hernández
The Ohio State University, Moritz College of Law
A leading national voice on immigration law and policy, Professor García Hernández is the author of Welcome the Wretched and Migrating to Prison. His scholarship examines the intersection of criminal and immigration law and the human impact of enforcement policy.

Symposium Schedule

Panels & Presentations

The day features a keynote address and four expert panels examining mixed-status families from legal, scholarly, journalistic, and community perspectives. Times are approximate; final schedule to follow.

Morning · Opening
Keynote Address
Professor César Cuauhtémoc García Hernández, The Ohio State University, Moritz College of Law
Morning · Panel 1
The Legal & Scholarly Landscape
Featuring leading scholars examining mixed-status families through legal and academic frameworks, including perspectives from immigration law, constitutional rights, and transborder studies.
Midday · Panel 2
Public Narratives & Media Perspectives
A dialogue with journalists, advocates, and community leaders on how mixed-status families are represented — and misrepresented — in public discourse and media coverage.
Afternoon · Panel 3
Diplomacy, Consular Protection & Binational Realities
Exploring the roles of consular offices, binational organizations, and international perspectives in supporting mixed-status families and transborder communities.
Afternoon · Panel 4
Community Response: Legal Services, Advocacy & Resistance
Practitioners and community organizers discuss frontline legal services, know-your-rights efforts, and grassroots strategies supporting families under threat.
Moderated by Professor Evelyn H. Cruz, ASU Law
